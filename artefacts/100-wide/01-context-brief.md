# Context Brief — European Insurance Cloud Migration

**Case A** · Meridian Retail Group · 2026-06-17

---

## Market Trends

**1. Softening Pricing Cycle & Capital Inflow**
Global P/C underwriting is in a softening phase — property rates declined 9% in Q1 2026 (Marsh), global insured catastrophe losses ran 26% below the 10-year average (Gallagher Re, Q1 2026), and new capital is flowing into the sector. Berkshire Hathaway CEO Greg Abel cited an "increasingly competitive" environment. In Europe, the same dynamics are pressuring premium rates across commercial lines, squeezing legacy carriers with higher cost bases.

*Sources: Insurance Journal (May 2026), Gallagher Re (Q1 2026), Marsh (Q1 2026)*

**2. Technology-Driven Competitive Divergence**
Progressive overtook GEICO as #2 US auto carrier (18.6% vs 11.56% share) by investing in telematics (Snapshot) and AI-driven pricing two decades earlier. GEICO's intentional volume pullback (2022–2024) restored margins but sacrificed growth — Abel now warns "it's not going to be easy to just restart the growth engine." In Europe, Allianz deepened its CMT telematics partnership in 2026, and usage-based insurance is the primary competitive battleground. *Hypothesis: carriers still running batch-pricing on legacy infrastructure (as Kepler is) face accelerating market share loss to tech-native competitors.*

*Sources: NAIC data (2025 filings), Insurance Journal (May 2026), SimplyWall.St / Allianz CMT (2026)*

**3. Consumer Price Sensitivity at Record Levels**
53% of US auto insurance customers shopped for a new policy (J.D. Power 2026 Study); 47.3% annual shop rate (LexisNexis Q1 2026). European markets show similar trends — comparison platforms (Compare the Market, Verivox) report increased switching frequency post-2024 rate increases. Price sensitivity is structural, not cyclical: consumers now integrate insurance cost into vehicle purchasing decisions (56% do, per LexisNexis 2026 Trends Report).

*Sources: J.D. Power (June 2026), LexisNexis (Q1 2026 / 2026 Trends Report), TransUnion (Q4 2025)*

## Regulatory Shifts (European Focus)

- **EIOPA AI Principles (2025–2026)** — European insurance regulator published principles for AI governance in underwriting and claims, requiring explainability and bias testing — binding through national transposition
- **DORA (Digital Operational Resilience Act) — in force Jan 2025** — mandatory ICT incident reporting, resilience testing, and third-party risk management for all EU financial entities. Directly relevant to a cloud migration: any migration plan must demonstrate operational resilience continuity
- **UK FCA Consumer Duty (ongoing)** — firm obligation to evidence fair value outcomes; legacy pricing systems that can't segment or explain rates are a compliance risk
- **GDPR enforcement wave (2025–2026)** — multiple European DPA fines on insurers for inadequate data processing basis in telematics and underwriting models (CNIL, ICO, DPC)
- **Solvency II review (2026)** — European Commission's 2026 review includes digital resilience and climate scenario requirements
- **NAIC developments (US, but trend-setting)** — 12-state pilot of AI Systems Evaluation Tool (March–Sept 2026); proposed third-party vendor registration for AI/ML in pricing, underwriting, claims. European regulators are watching.

*Sources: EIOPA (2025–2026), EU DORA Regulation 2022/2554, FCA Handbook (2025–2026), NAIC Spring Meeting summaries (Fenwick & West, Foley & Lardner, Mitchell Williams — 2026)*

## Three Strategic Pain Points

### Pain Point 1: Legacy Tech Cost Base Is Unsustainable in a Soft Market
Operating 50 applications on dual AWS + on-prem footprints inflates infrastructure costs at exactly the wrong moment — pricing power is eroding market-wide. **Evidence:** Marsh global rate decline -9% (Q1 2026); Abel warning on margin compression (May 2026).

### Pain Point 2: Regulatory Risk Peaks During Migration Without DORA-Compliant Resilience Planning
DORA's ICT resilience testing and third-party risk management requirements (in force Jan 2025) mean the 18-month migration timeline has zero tolerance for gaps in operational continuity. 11 orphaned applications (no current owner) are a material DORA compliance exposure. **Evidence:** DORA Regulation 2022/2554; EIOPA 2025–2026 AI Principles; GDPR fines trajectory (CNIL, ICO 2025–2026).

### Pain Point 3: AI/Telematics Competitors Will Widen the Gap During the Pause
Every month spent migrating is a month competitors (Allianz via CMT, Progressive-style telematics plays) improve their AI-driven pricing and capture switching customers. 53% shopping rate means customers won't wait. **Evidence:** J.D. Power (June 2026); NAIC market share data (2025); Allianz/CMT deal (SimplyWall.St, 2026).
